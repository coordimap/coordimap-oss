## Design Context

### Users
Platform and SRE practitioners tracing stored infrastructure relationships during incident investigation, plus engineers reviewing architecture and dependencies in an MCP Apps-capable host. They need to orient quickly around one asset without treating stored observations as live state.

### Brand Personality
Technical, confident, and signal-rich. The interface should feel precise and calm while making relationship structure immediately legible.

### Aesthetic Direction
Obsidian signal: a visually impactful, 3D-like two-dimensional topology rendered in a host-controlled sandbox. Use a deep obsidian canvas, pale high-contrast labels, cyan root emphasis, violet second-layer accents, and semantic status colors only as secondary signals. Avoid actual 3D, external assets, generic dashboard cards, and decorative motion unrelated to relationships.

### Design Principles
- Make bounded stored topology and unresolved endpoints explicit; never imply live state or missing facts.
- Preserve fast orientation through a fixed root, depth cues, concise technical labels, and predictable controls.
- Use motion only to communicate graph relationships; honor reduced-motion preferences completely.
- Keep non-canvas controls keyboard-accessible with clear focus states and touch targets of at least 44 CSS pixels.
- Adapt the inspection surface to narrow embeds without hiding graph, details, or node navigation.
